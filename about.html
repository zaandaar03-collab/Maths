About Formula 1

Formula 1 is the highest class of international single-seater auto racing, governed by the FIA. It represents the ultimate combination of speed, engineering, strategy, and driver skill.

Modern F1 cars use hybrid turbocharged engines capable of exceeding 350 km/h. The championship consists of multiple Grand Prix events held across the world.

Teams invest heavily in aerodynamics, data analysis, tire strategy, and pit stop precision, making F1 one of the most technologically advanced sports on Earth.
